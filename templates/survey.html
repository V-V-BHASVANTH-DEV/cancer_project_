Smoking (Range: 0 - 2)

- Description: Indicates the smoking habit of an individual.
- 0: Non-smoker.
- 1: Occasional smoker.
- 2: Regular smoker.
- Smoking is a well-known risk factor for various cancers, including lung cancer.

Alcohol Consumption (Range: 0 - 3)

- Description: Reflects the level of alcohol consumption.
- 0: Non-drinker.
- 1-3: Increasing levels of alcohol consumption.
- High alcohol intake is linked to an increased risk of certain cancers, such as liver cancer.

Red Meat Consumption (Range: 0 - 2)

- Description: Assesses the frequency of red meat consumption.
- 0: Rarely or never eats red meat.
- 1: Eats red meat occasionally.
- 2: Frequently consumes red meat.
- Higher consumption of red meat has been associated with an increased risk of certain cancers.

Exposure to Pollution (Range: 0 - 1)

- Description: Indicates the level of exposure to pollution.
- 0: Low or no exposure.
- 1: High exposure.
- Pollution, especially air pollution, has been linked to increased cancer risk.

Autoimmune Disorder (Range: 0 - 1)

- Description: Indicates whether the individual has an autoimmune disorder.
- 0: No autoimmune disorder.
- 1: Presence of an autoimmune disorder.
- Certain autoimmune disorders may increase the risk of cancer.

Cancer Risk Prediction Form

Diabetes History (Range: 0 - 1)

- Description: Reflects whether the individual has a history of diabetes.
- 0: No history of diabetes.
- 1: Has diabetes.
- Diabetes has been linked to a higher risk of some types of cancer.

Exercise (Range: 0 - 3)

- Description: Measures the frequency of exercise.
- 0: No exercise.
- 1-3: Increasing levels of physical activity.
- Regular physical activity is known to reduce the risk of several cancers.

Fruits and Veggies Consumption (Range: 0 - 2)

- Description: Indicates the consumption level of fruits and vegetables.
- 0: Low consumption.
- 1: Moderate consumption.
- 2: High consumption.
- A diet rich in fruits and vegetables is associated with a lower risk of cancer.

Sleep Hours (Range: 4 - 12)

- Description: The average number of sleep hours per night.
- Adequate sleep is important for overall health and may influence cancer risk.

Mental Health (Range: 0 - 3)

- Description: Reflects the state of the individual's mental health.
- 0: Good mental health.
- 1-3: Increasing levels of mental health concerns.
- While the direct link between mental health and cancer risk is complex, overall well-being, including mental health, plays a role in physical health.
